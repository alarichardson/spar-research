03About

Why middle power leverage matters

Most research on AI geopolitics focuses on the US-China dyad. But the trajectory of the AI race will also be shaped by the actions of middle powers — states with significant but not dominant influence — whose decisions on coalitions, capital, supply chains, and regulation could meaningfully accelerate or slow risky dynamics.

This project, part of the SPAR Spring 2026 cohort, produces short, analysis-driven investigations into where that leverage actually exists, how large it is, and how it could be directed toward AI safety. Our goal is to give policymakers, philanthropists, and researchers concrete, actionable answers.

Get in Touch

Leverage-First Framing

We don't ask what middle powers should do in theory — we ask what leverage they actually hold, and what actions are realistic given their goals, resources, and bottlenecks.

Short-Form, Actionable Outputs

Each investigation runs 2 weeks to 2 months and is designed to produce findings that policymakers, philanthropists, and AI labs can act on directly.

Mixed Methods

We combine desk research and document review with structured interviews across governments, sovereign wealth funds, and AI companies — then test findings in small convenings.
